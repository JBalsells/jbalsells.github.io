About

Jorge Balsells is an Electronic Engineer with a Master's degree in Mathematical Engineering and Computing and a Master's degree in Artificial Intelligence. With more than 10 years of experience across firmware development, software engineering, and machine learning operations, his work focuses on connecting hardware and software to build reliable and data-driven systems.

This multidisciplinary background provides a strong foundation for solving complex engineering problems, ranging from embedded systems and signal processing to the design and deployment of scalable machine learning infrastructure in production environments.

He currently serves as a professor at the School of Physical Sciences and Mathematics (ECFM) at Universidad de San Carlos de Guatemala, where he teaches Simulation and Instrumentation laboratories. He also contributes as a development engineer to the LAGO project (Latin American Giant Observatory).

In parallel, he works as a Machine Learning Operations Engineer at Vana.gt, contributing to the development and maintenance of infrastructure for training, deploying, and monitoring machine learning models at scale for the Data Science Risk team.

Outside of engineering, he serves as a volunteer firefighter, with particular interest in fire suppression and rope systems for safe work at height and technical rescue. This experience reinforces discipline, teamwork, and the ability to operate effectively in demanding and high-responsibility situations.

Fuera del ámbito profesional, participa como bombero voluntario, con especial interés en el combate de incendios y en sistemas de cuerdas para trabajo seguro en alturas y rescate técnico. Esta actividad fortalece la disciplina, el trabajo en equipo y la capacidad de actuar con responsabilidad en situaciones exigentes.
